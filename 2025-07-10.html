Luis Sojo

Career Highlights & Facts

  • Luis Sojo was a versatile infielder, primarily playing second base, shortstop, and third base, making him a valuable utility player throughout his career.
  • He won four World Series championships as a player with the New York Yankees (1996, 1998, 1999, 2000).
  • Sojo famously hit the game-winning single in Game 5 of the 2000 World Series against the New York Mets, clinching the championship for the Yankees.
  • After his playing career, he transitioned into coaching, serving as a coach for the New York Yankees and managing in their minor league system, as well as managing the Venezuelan national baseball team in the World Baseball Classic.

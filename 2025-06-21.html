George Stirnweiss "Snuffy"

Career Highlights & Facts

  • In 1945, he led the American League in multiple offensive categories, including hits (195), triples (22), runs (107), and stolen bases (33).
  • He finished in the top 10 for the American League Most Valuable Player award in both 1944 (9th) and 1945 (5th).
  • His baseball career was interrupted by service in the United States Army Air Forces during World War II.
  • Tragically, Stirnweiss died in a commuter train accident in New York in 1958 at the age of 39, just six years after his retirement from baseball.

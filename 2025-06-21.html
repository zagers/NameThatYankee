George Henry Stirnweiss "Snuffy"

Career Highlights & Facts

  • In 1944, Snuffy Stirnweiss led the American League in four major offensive categories: batting average (.319), hits (205), runs (125), and stolen bases (55).
  • He was a member of four New York Yankees World Series championship teams (1943, 1947, 1949, 1950).
  • Stirnweiss was selected to the American League All-Star team in 1946.
  • His 55 stolen bases in 1944 were the most by an American League player since Ty Cobb stole 96 in 1915, and the most by any AL player for over a decade afterward.

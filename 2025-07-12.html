Jay Johnstone

Career Highlights & Facts

  • Jay Johnstone won two World Series championships during his career: one with the New York Yankees in 1978 and another with the Los Angeles Dodgers in 1981.
  • He was widely known throughout his career as one of baseball's most notorious clubhouse pranksters, often engaging in elaborate practical jokes on his teammates.
  • Following his playing career, Johnstone transitioned into broadcasting, serving as a color commentator for both the New York Yankees and the Los Angeles Dodgers.
  • He co-authored two books, 'Temporary Insanity' and 'Over the Edge,' which chronicled his experiences in baseball and his reputation for comical antics.

James Anthony Abbott

Career Highlights & Facts

  • Born without a right hand, he is one of the few players to reach the Major Leagues despite his physical challenge, successfully adapting his pitching and fielding technique.
  • Pitched a no-hitter for the New York Yankees against the Cleveland Indians on September 4, 1993, at Yankee Stadium.
  • He was drafted in the first round (eighth overall) of the 1988 Major League Baseball draft and made his MLB debut without playing in the minor leagues.
  • Abbott won the Golden Spikes Award in 1987 as the top amateur baseball player and earned an Olympic gold medal with the U.S. baseball team at the 1988 Summer Olympics.

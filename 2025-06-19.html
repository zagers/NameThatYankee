Jim Abbott

Career Highlights & Facts

  • Jim Abbott was born without a right hand, making him one of the most remarkable pitchers in baseball history to achieve success at the Major League level despite the physical challenge.
  • On September 4, 1993, while pitching for the New York Yankees, Abbott threw a no-hitter against the Cleveland Indians at Yankee Stadium.
  • Before making his MLB debut without ever playing a minor league game, Abbott was an Olympic gold medalist, winning with the United States baseball team at the 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul, South Korea.
  • In 1987, Abbott was awarded the James E. Sullivan Award as the top amateur athlete in the United States, becoming the first baseball player ever to receive the honor.

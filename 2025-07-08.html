Jay Campbell Buhner "Bone"

Career Highlights & Facts

  • Jay Buhner was famously traded by the New York Yankees to the Seattle Mariners on July 21, 1988, in a deal for Ken Phelps, a transaction later humorously referenced on the TV show Seinfeld by Frank Costanza.
  • Buhner was a pivotal power hitter for the Seattle Mariners during the mid-1990s, forming a formidable lineup alongside stars like Ken Griffey Jr. and Edgar Martínez, leading the team to its first-ever division title in 1995.
  • He is one of only three players in Seattle Mariners franchise history to hit 300 or more home runs with the team, accumulating 307 of his 310 career homers in a Mariners uniform.
  • Buhner was known for his distinctive shaved head look, which became a fan favorite and led to promotional events and fan merchandise, further cementing his iconic status in Seattle.

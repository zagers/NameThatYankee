Daryl Boston

Career Highlights & Facts

  • Daryl Boston was drafted by the Chicago White Sox in the first round (4th overall) of the 1981 Major League Baseball draft.
  • He played for four different MLB teams during his career: the Chicago White Sox, New York Mets, Colorado Rockies, and New York Yankees.
  • Boston was a member of the inaugural Colorado Rockies team in 1993, hitting .256 with 7 home runs in 114 games.
  • His final Major League season was 1994 with the New York Yankees, which was notably shortened by the players' strike.
